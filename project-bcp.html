Overview

The Challenge

At the height of the COVID-19 pandemic in Peru, critical health data — ICU bed availability, oxygen supplies, COVID testing centres — was scattered across dozens of government sources. Citizens had no single, clear way to access the information they needed to make life-or-death decisions.

BCP (Banco de Crédito del Perú), the country's largest bank, partnered with OpenCovidPeru to host a 48-hour hackathon challenging teams to build solutions that made COVID-19 data accessible and actionable for everyday Peruvians.

The problem: Complex government datasets were inaccessible to ordinary citizens. People didn't know where to find the nearest available ICU bed, oxygen supply point, or COVID health centre in their region.

Methodology

How We Built It

01
Research & Data Analysis
Analysed government COVID-19 open datasets to identify what health resource data was available by region: ICU beds, COVID beds, oxygen cylinders, pharmacies, and health centres.
02
Design Thinking Workshop
Facilitated a full-team ideation session to align on user needs — who was most affected, what decisions they were trying to make, and what form of visualisation would be most useful.
03
Qualitative Research
Conducted rapid user interviews and questionnaires to validate assumptions about citizen needs — ensuring the solution addressed real behaviours, not assumed ones.
04
UX & Data Visualisation
Designed the interactive map of Peru with regional health data layers, prioritising clarity, speed of access, and accessibility — so even low-digital-literacy users could find information quickly.
The Solution

Platform Features

  • Interactive Peru Map: Filterable, regional map showing ICU beds, COVID beds, oxygen cylinders, pharmacies and health centres in real time
  • Data Dashboard: Clean statistics display with COVID-19 case trends, hospitalisations, and resource availability at a national level
  • Citizen-Friendly Language: Complex data rewritten in plain language, avoiding medical jargon
  • Mobile-Optimised: Designed for accessibility on low-spec devices common in Peru's most affected regions
Design principle: Every design decision was tested against the question — "can someone in crisis, under stress, find this information in under 30 seconds?" That constraint drove the simplicity of the interface.
Impact

Results & Recognition

Within 48 hours, the team delivered a working platform that was immediately accepted, awarded, and put into public use — reaching citizens at a critical moment in Peru's pandemic response.
