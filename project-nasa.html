Overview

The Challenge

NASA's Space Apps Challenge is one of the world's largest global hackathons, bringing together thousands of teams to solve real-world problems using open data. In 2021, our challenge was to leverage AI and Machine Learning to address plastic marine debris — one of the most pressing environmental crises of our time.

Over 8 million tonnes of plastic enter our oceans every year. Monitoring and quantifying this pollution at scale requires tools that go beyond human capacity — satellite imagery, computer vision, and intelligent data systems.

The problem: There was no accessible, integrated platform for citizens, researchers, and organisations to detect, monitor, and act on plastic pollution in coastal areas using real-time satellite data.

The Solution

Platform Features

  • Image Upload & Detection: Citizens can upload photos of contaminated coastal areas; the computer vision model identifies and classifies plastic debris
  • Global Heatmap: Real-time visualisation of plastic pollution hotspots using NASA satellite data
  • Historical Data: Time-series data allowing researchers to track pollution changes over time
  • Virtual Assistant: NLP-powered chatbot that educates users, guides reporting, and answers environmental queries
Why it mattered: Track the Plastic was one of the first platforms to make NASA's open satellite data actionable for both researchers and everyday citizens — democratising environmental monitoring in a way that existing tools had not.
