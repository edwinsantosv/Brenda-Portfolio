Overview

The Challenge

Co-op is the UK's fifth largest food retailer — a company with a strong heritage in community values and ethical trading. As sustainability pressures intensified across the retail sector, Co-op tasked a team of University of Leeds Business Master's students to develop a strategic consultancy proposal to enhance their sustainability practices and position Co-op as the best in UK retail.

The brief required not just an understanding of Co-op's current position, but a rigorous competitive analysis across Waitrose, Tesco, Sainsbury's, Aldi, M&S, and Asda — and a structured, commercially grounded roadmap aligned with the UK Government's Net Zero commitments.

The brief: Develop a strategic consultancy proposal to position Co-op as the sustainability leader in UK food retail, using evidence-based analysis and a structured business framework applicable to a FTSE-level company.

Methodology

The Approach

01
Regulatory Landscape
Analysed the UK Government's Net Zero targets and sustainability legislation affecting the food retail sector — understanding the compliance baseline and areas where Co-op could lead rather than follow.
02
Competitive Analysis
Mapped the sustainability positioning, initiatives, and public commitments of Waitrose, Tesco, Sainsbury's, Aldi, M&S, and Asda — identifying where Co-op had differentiation opportunities.
03
Consumer Research
Studied consumer behaviour and sustainability attitudes by generation — understanding how Gen Z, Millennials, Gen X, and Boomers differ in their purchasing decisions and what drives each cohort's sustainability engagement.
04
Strategic Recommendations
Applied the Three Horizons Framework to structure recommendations across three time horizons — ensuring Co-op had a credible short-term plan, a medium-term differentiation strategy, and a long-term sustainability vision.
Framework

Three Horizons Framework

The Three Horizons Framework, as used by McKinsey & Company, structures strategy across three time horizons — ensuring organisations balance immediate performance with longer-term transformation.

Horizon 1 · Now
Defend & Extend
Optimise Co-op's existing sustainability initiatives — packaging reduction, food waste programmes, and supplier standards — to consolidate their current position.
Horizon 2 · Emerging
Build New Strengths
Develop new sustainability capabilities — community-led sourcing, circular economy pilots, and consumer-facing sustainability transparency tools — to differentiate from competitors.
Horizon 3 · Future
Create Tomorrow
Position Co-op as the definitive sustainability leader in UK retail — building systemic partnerships, driving industry standards, and aligning with Net Zero ambitions across the full supply chain.
